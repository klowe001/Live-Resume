## How I Think

### Strategy Through Execution

The most elegant strategy is worthless without the ability to ship. I've spent years translating business intent into engineering specs, financial models, and operating rhythms that actually move organizations. Now I build products myself to close the loop entirely.

- Strategy without execution is theater
- The gap between "recommendation" and "shipped outcome" is where value is created or destroyed
- I've sat on both sides: the consultant who frames the decision and the builder who makes it real

### AI as Amplifier

AI accelerates judgment. I use agentic workflows to prototype in hours what once took weeks, but the value still comes from knowing which problems to solve and how to shape solutions that people actually want.

- AI is a force multiplier for people who already know what to build
- The bottleneck has shifted from "can we build it?" to "should we build it?"
- I use Claude Code, Replit Agent, and other tools not as novelties but as core workflow
- The companies that win will have leaders who understand both the strategic context and the technical implementation

### Economics-First GTM

Unit economics, pricing mechanics, loyalty systems‚ÄîI've worked at the intersection of customer experience and P&L impact for Fortune 500 companies. Every product decision should connect to a measurable outcome.

- Loyalty programs are financial instruments with marketing attached
- Pricing is the most underleveraged strategic lever most companies have
- I model revenue lift, redemption liability, and unit economics before recommending action
- GTM is how the product meets reality

### Player-Coach Mentality

I go hands-on where it reduces iteration cycles or unblocks others. Build the first version, document the pattern, then coach someone to own v2 and v3. Success means the team can ship without me.

- The best leaders know when to step in and when to step back
- "Player-coach" means deciding what to do yourself vs. delegate based on where you create the most leverage
- I measure success by whether the team can ship without me
- Teaching is scaling. When I transfer a capability, I've multiplied my impact.
